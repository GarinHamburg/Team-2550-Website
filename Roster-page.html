Also organized by time bio received.

Caleb (Student Mentor)

ROLES:  Programming & Electrical Mentor, and Technical Documentation

Caleb’s interest in FIRST started at the beginning of middle school, where he joined the Canby FLL team. he contributed there for two years and was one of the primary programmers and builders. Throughout the build season, he would take the robot home and work late into the night on refining the robot. His interest was propelled by a mentor in the FLL team. When he got into high school, he could not join a robotics team for various reasons. In the 2013 season, he was able to join this team. That year, he did basically anything that needed to be done - he made various small contributions to the robot construction. In 2013-2014 season, most of the team members were new. Due to previous experience, he was chosen to help head the programming team. He did a significant portion of the wiring and programming on the robot.

    Currently, he is working on documenting the various things that he have learned this year. It is available here (currently under construction). He plans to include information about C++, pneumatics, electrical systems, possibly some mechanical systems, motor controllers, and sensors. Looking into the future, he is heading toward electrical and computer engineering.

Debbie (Mentor)

ROLES: Marketing/Community Outreach Mentor

 Debbie has been involved with FIRST since 2007 when her oldest son joined FLL. She began as a parent volunteer for her son’s FLL team. Since that time, Debbie helped organize and coach a rookie Jr.FLL team and a rookie FLL team. When her oldest transitioned to a rookie FTC team and a rookie FRC team sponsored by Oregon City School District, she was involved as a parent volunteer. At its first Portland Autodesk Regional, Team 2550 won Rookie All-Star and advanced to worlds in Atlanta, Georgia. Debbie supported the team at worlds twice, and caught the vision and the mission of FIRST. The past year 2013-2014 she coached a Rookie FTC team #8191. Debbie is currently a FIRST Senior Mentor for Oregon. She graduated from BYU in 1994 with a Bachelor of Science and a minor in Business.
Shannan (Mentor)


ROLES: Marketing Mentor

Shannan was introduced to FIRST in 2013/14 when her son Jared joined Team 2550. She was very impressed with the mission of FIRST and the excitement the organization brought to students in the fields of engineering and industrial sciences, and how they incorporated the business process into the program.
